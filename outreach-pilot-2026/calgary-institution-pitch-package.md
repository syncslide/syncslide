## Time-Sensitive Context: Regulatory Pressure Is Real Right Now

ADA Title II amendments require US public universities to make their digital course content accessible by **April 24, 2026** — 25 days from the date this package was written. Canadian institutions are not directly subject to ADA, but:

- The AODA (Ontario) and equivalent provincial human rights frameworks carry similar obligations
- US-affiliated campuses and institutions with cross-border partnerships are directly in scope
- The practical compliance challenge is identical: existing slide decks are not readable by screen readers
- When a US peer institution gets a human rights complaint over inaccessible slides, Canadian institutions notice

**Lead with this in conversation:** "Universities are scrambling right now. The tools that help make presentations accessible aren't keeping up with demand. We want to work with you before this becomes a crisis rather than after."

No claims about legal compliance should ever be made. This is about the tool helping accessibility coordinators do their job.

---

## The Problem We Solve

Lecture slides are the primary learning material in most courses. Screen reader users — students who are blind or have low vision — cannot access most slide decks:

- PowerPoint files exported to PDF lose reading order
- Uploaded PDFs lack proper heading structure, alt text, and semantic markup
- Faculty have no easy way to produce accessible slides without specialized training

Manual remediation from established vendors costs **$4–8 per slide**. A mid-size university produces 1.2 to 6 million slides per semester. That's not a realistic budget for anyone.

**What SyncSlide does:** Faculty create presentations in Markdown. SyncSlide renders them with semantic HTML — heading structure, keyboard navigation, screen reader announcements, and live captions built in. No retrofitting. No per-slide cost.

**What we do NOT claim:**
- We do not guarantee WCAG compliance of the final output — that depends on what the instructor writes
- We do not provide legal coverage or reduce legal risk
- We do not claim to replace accessibility reviews for existing content
